What is Girls Who Code?

The Girls Who Code Clubs Program offers computer science education to 6th-12th grade girls nationally. Girls meet weekly and are taught by volunteer instructors trained on the Girls Who Code curriculum. In addition to learning CS, Club participants get exclusive access to Girls Who Code challenges, events, and speakers.

Girls Who Code Bothell is a local GWC club that meets in the Bothell, WA Library. The club is open to all students, grades 6-12, with a max limit of 20 students.

  • Eligibility: Open to all students, grades 6-12, with a max limit of 20 students.
  • Meetings: Thursdays, 3:30pm-5:30pm in the Bothell Library.
  • Cost: Membership, instruction, and software is provided free of charge. Students are encouraged to bring their own laptop if one is available. If not, one will be provided for them for use during club meetings.
  • Attendance: Club members are expected to attend at least 80% of club meetings.
  • How to join: Contact the club advisor on the contact page.
